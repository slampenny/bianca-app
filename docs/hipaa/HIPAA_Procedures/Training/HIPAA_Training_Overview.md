## MODULE 1: HIPAA BASICS (10 minutes)

### What is HIPAA?

**HIPAA** = Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (1996)

**Purpose**:
- Protect patient privacy
- Ensure security of health information
- Give patients rights over their health data
- Establish national standards for healthcare

**Who Must Comply?**:
- Healthcare providers (doctors, hospitals, etc.)
- Health plans (insurance companies)
- Healthcare clearinghouses
- **Business Associates** â† This is us!

**What We Do**:
MyPhoneFriend provides communication services for healthcare organizations. We process Protected Health Information (PHI) on behalf of healthcare providers, making us a "Business Associate" under HIPAA.

---

### Why HIPAA Matters

**For Patients**:
- Privacy of sensitive health information
- Control over who sees their data
- Trust in healthcare system

**For Our Company**:
- Legal requirement (not optional)
- Customer trust and contracts
- Avoid massive fines
- Professional reputation

**Penalties for Violations**:
- **Individual**: Up to $250,000 in fines + criminal charges
- **Company**: Up to $1.9 million per violation per year
- **You personally**: Can be held liable for willful neglect

**Real Examples**:
- UCLA Health: $865,000 fine (employees snooping on celebrity records)
- Anthem: $16 million fine (data breach affecting 79 million)
- Individual doctor: 4 months in prison (unauthorized access)

---

### HIPAA Rules

**Privacy Rule** (45 CFR Part 160 and 164):
- Controls use and disclosure of PHI
- Gives patients rights to their data
- Minimum necessary standard

**Security Rule** (45 CFR Part 164.308-312):
- Protects electronic PHI (ePHI)
- Administrative safeguards
- Physical safeguards
- Technical safeguards

**Breach Notification Rule**:
- Notify individuals of breaches
- Report breaches to HHS
- Timeline: 60 days

## MODULE 2: PROTECTED HEALTH INFORMATION (15 minutes)

### What is PHI?

**PHI** = Any health information that can be linked to an individual

**18 Identifiers** (must remove to de-identify):
1. Names
2. Geographic subdivisions smaller than state
3. Dates (except year)
4. Phone numbers
5. Fax numbers
6. Email addresses
7. Social Security numbers
8. Medical record numbers
9. Health plan numbers
10. Account numbers
11. Certificate/license numbers
12. Vehicle IDs and serial numbers
13. Device IDs and serial numbers
14. URLs
15. IP addresses
16. Biometric identifiers
17. Full-face photos
18. Any other unique identifying number/characteristic

**Plus**: Any health information (diagnoses, treatments, medications, test results, etc.)

---

### PHI in Our System

**Examples of PHI We Handle**:
- âœ… Patient names, emails, phone numbers
- âœ… Patient addresses and dates of birth
- âœ… Conversation transcripts between patients and AI assistant
- âœ… Medical analysis and sentiment analysis results
- âœ… Emergency alerts and notifications
- âœ… Call recordings and voicemail
- âœ… Medical notes and diagnoses

**NOT PHI in Our System**:
- âŒ Your employee information (HIPAA doesn't cover employees)
- âŒ Anonymized/de-identified data (once all 18 identifiers removed)
- âŒ System configuration data
- âŒ Aggregate statistics (no individual linkage)

---

### How to Handle PHI

**Minimum Necessary Rule**:
Only access the minimum PHI needed to do your job.

**Examples**:
- âœ… GOOD: Customer support accessing patient's contact info to assist
- âŒ BAD: Reading patient conversations out of curiosity
- âœ… GOOD: Reviewing your assigned patients' alerts
- âŒ BAD: Accessing patient records of friends/family

**Golden Rule**: If you don't need it for your job, don't access it.


**How to Protect**:
- âœ… Verify sender email carefully
- âœ… Never click suspicious links
- âœ… Report to security@biancawellness.com
- âœ… When in doubt, don't click

**2. Social Engineering**
**What**: Tricking people into revealing information

**Example**:

**How to Protect**:
- âœ… Never give passwords to anyone

- âœ… Report suspicious calls/visits

**3. Malware and Ransomware**

**How to Protect**:
- âœ… Don't download unknown files
- âœ… Don't click email attachments from unknown senders
- âœ… Keep antivirus updated
- âœ… Report suspicious pop-ups
- âœ… Report if computer behaves strangely

**4. Lost/Stolen Devices**

**How to Protect**:
- âœ… Enable full disk encryption (required)
- âœ… Use strong device password
- âœ… Enable remote wipe (company devices)

- âœ… Report loss immediately

**5. Insider Threats**
**What**: Employees accessing PHI inappropriately

**Examples**:

**How to Protect**:

## MODULE 5: TECHNICAL SAFEGUARDS (10 minutes)

### Security Controls in Our System

**1. Authentication**
- JWT tokens for secure login
- Passwords hashed with bcrypt (unrecoverable)
- MFA available (required for admins)
- Session timeout after 15 minutes idle

**What You Do**:
- Create strong passwords
- Enable MFA if available
- Log out when finished
- Never share credentials

---

**2. Audit Logging**
- Every action logged automatically
- Logs are tamper-proof (cannot be deleted or modified)
- Logs kept for 7 years
- Regular reviews by Security Officer

**What This Means**:
- Every patient you view is logged
- Every update you make is logged
- Who you are, what you did, when you did it
- If you do something wrong, we will know

**Your Responsibility**:
- Assume all access is monitored (it is)
- Only access what you need for your job
- You can be held accountable for unauthorized access

---

**3. Breach Detection**
- Automated monitoring for suspicious activity
- 4 detection rules running continuously
  - Excessive failed logins (5+ in 5 minutes)
  - Unusual data access volume (100+ records/hour)
  - Rapid data access (20+ records/minute)
  - Off-hours access (10 PM - 7 AM)
- Automatic account lockout
- Security Officer notified immediately

**What This Means**:
- System watches for unusual behavior
- Your account will be locked if suspicious
- You'll be contacted to explain
- Intentional violations = termination

---

**4. Access Controls**
- Role-based permissions (you only see what your role allows)
- Minimum necessary filtering (extra fields hidden based on role)
- Cannot access patients not assigned to you (if staff)
- IP address restrictions (if configured)

**Your Role**:
- **staff**: Limited patient info, assigned patients only
- **orgAdmin**: More info, all patients in your organization
- **superAdmin**: Full access (extreme caution required)

---

**5. Encryption**
- All data encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS)
- Database encrypted at rest (AES-256)
- Passwords hashed (bcrypt)
- MFA secrets encrypted
- Backups encrypted

**What This Means**:
- Data is protected even if systems compromised
- Encrypted backups stored securely
- You still must protect your credentials

## MODULE 6: PATIENT RIGHTS (5 minutes)

### Patients Have the Right To:

**1. Access Their Records**
- View their own PHI
- Get copies of their records
- We must respond within 30 days

**2. Request Corrections**
- Correct inaccurate information
- Add clarifications to records
- We must respond within 60 days

**3. Accounting of Disclosures**
- Know who accessed their records (last 6 years)
- We provide audit log summary
- Excludes treatment, payment, operations (unless requested)

**4. Request Restrictions**
- Ask to limit use/disclosure
- We may decline if affects care
- Must honor if self-paid

**5. Confidential Communications**
- Request communications via specific method
- Reasonable requests must be honored

**6. Privacy Practices Notice**
- Receive Notice of Privacy Practices
- Explains how we use their PHI

**How to Handle Patient Requests**:
1. Listen politely
2. Get request in writing
3. Forward to Security Officer
4. Don't promise what you can't deliver
5. We have 30-60 days to respond (depending on type)
