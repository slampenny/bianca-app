## 1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes sanctions for workforce members who violate HIPAA security and privacy policies, procedures, or the HIPAA regulations themselves, in compliance with ยง164.308(a)(1)(ii)(C).

---

## 2. POLICY STATEMENT

MyPhoneFriend will apply appropriate sanctions against workforce members who fail to comply with HIPAA security and privacy policies and procedures. Sanctions will be applied fairly and consistently, with disciplinary action proportional to the severity and intent of the violation.

---

## 3. SCOPE

This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time, part-time)
- Contractors and temporary workers
- Interns and volunteers
- Consultants with access to ePHI
- Any workforce member with system access

---

## 4. VIOLATION CATEGORIES

### 4.1 SEVERITY LEVELS

**Level 1: MINOR** (Unintentional, low risk)
- First-time policy violation
- No ePHI disclosure
- Immediately self-reported
- Minimal security impact

**Examples**:
- Forgot to lock screen (once)
- Brief password sharing (corrected immediately)
- Accidental click on phishing email (reported)
- Policy procedure not followed (no harm)

**Sanction**: Verbal warning + retraining

---

**Level 2: MODERATE** (Carelessness, medium risk)
- Repeated minor violations
- Small ePHI exposure
- Policy violation with some risk
- Not immediately reported

**Examples**:
- Multiple instances of unlocked screen
- Wrote password down (but secured)
- Accessed 1-2 unauthorized patient records (curiosity)
- Lost unencrypted device with minimal ePHI
- Failed to report incident within required timeframe

**Sanction**: Written warning + mandatory retraining + 30-day probation

---

**Level 3: SERIOUS** (Reckless, high risk)
- Significant policy violations
- Substantial ePHI exposure
- Intentional disregard for procedures
- Failure to report known violations

**Examples**:
- Accessed 10+ unauthorized patient records
- Shared ePHI via unencrypted email
- Bypassed security controls
- Disabled audit logging
- Lost device with substantial ePHI
- Shared login credentials with others
- Deliberate policy violation

**Sanction**: Suspension (unpaid) + mandatory retraining + 90-day probation  
**OR**: Termination (depending on circumstances)

---

**Level 4: SEVERE** (Willful, critical risk)
- Intentional HIPAA violations
- Malicious ePHI disclosure
- Theft or sale of ePHI
- Sabotage of security systems
- Retaliation after report

**Examples**:
- Accessing celebrity/VIP records without authorization
- Selling patient data
- Stealing ePHI for competitor
- Providing ePHI to media
- Accessing ex-spouse's/family records
- Tampering with audit logs
- Disabling encryption
- Identity theft using patient data

**Sanction**: Immediate termination + law enforcement notification + potential criminal prosecution

---

## 5. PROGRESSIVE DISCIPLINE

### 5.1 Standard Progressive Approach

**For Most Violations** (Levels 1-2):

**First Violation**:
1. Verbal warning
2. Documented conversation
3. Remedial training required
4. Manager notification
5. Security Officer notification

**Second Violation** (within 12 months):
1. Written warning (in employee file)
2. Mandatory security training
3. 30-day probation
4. Weekly check-ins with manager
5. Enhanced monitoring of account activity

**Third Violation** (within 12 months):
1. Final written warning
2. 90-day probation
3. Loss of privileges (reduced access)
4. Escalation to HR and management
5. Termination considered

**Fourth Violation**:
1. Immediate termination

---

### 5.2 Immediate Termination (Level 4)

**No progressive discipline** for:
- Willful HIPAA violations
- Intentional unauthorized ePHI access
- ePHI disclosure to unauthorized parties
- Theft or sale of ePHI
- Tampering with security systems
- Criminal activity
- Retaliation for reporting violations

**Process**:
1. Investigation confirms intentional violation
2. Security Officer recommends termination
3. HR and Legal consulted
4. Executive approval obtained
5. Immediate termination executed
6. All access revoked within 1 hour
7. Law enforcement contacted (if criminal)
8. Breach notification (if ePHI disclosed)

## 6. INVESTIGATION PROCEDURE

### 6.1 Allegation of Violation

**When Violation Suspected**:

**Step 1: Report Received**
- Source: Manager, colleague, automated detection, patient complaint, audit review
- Documented: Incident report form
- Assigned: Security Officer

**Step 2: Preliminary Assessment**
- Severity: Level 1, 2, 3, or 4?
- Evidence: Audit logs, witness statements, system logs
- Urgency: Immediate action needed?

**Step 3: Investigation**
1. Gather all evidence (audit logs, witness statements)
2. Interview employee (give opportunity to explain)
3. Review employment history (past violations?)
4. Consult with HR and Legal (if serious)
5. Determine facts and intent

**Step 4: Determination**
- Was violation confirmed?
- What was intent? (unintentional vs. willful)
- What was impact? (actual harm vs. potential harm)
- Prior violations?
- Mitigating factors?

**Step 5: Sanction Decision**
- Sanction level determined
- Proportional to violation
- Consistent with past sanctions
- Approved by appropriate authority

**Timeline**: Complete within 5 business days (unless complex)

---

### 6.2 Employee Rights

**During Investigation**:
- Right to be informed of allegations
- Right to explain their actions
- Right to present mitigating evidence
- Right to union representation (if applicable)
- Fair and impartial investigation

**After Sanction**:
- Right to appeal (see Section 9)
- Written notification of sanction
- Clear remediation path (if not termination)
- Access to retraining resources

## 8. SPECIFIC VIOLATIONS AND SANCTIONS

### 8.1 Unauthorized PHI Access

**Violation**: Accessing patient records without business need

**Determination Factors**:
- Number of records accessed: _______________
- Relationship to patients (friend/family/celebrity): _______________
- Intent (curiosity vs. malicious): _______________
- Duration/frequency: _______________

**Typical Sanctions**:
- 1-2 records, curiosity: Written warning + retraining
- 3-10 records: Suspension + probation
- 10+ records or malicious: Termination
- Family/celebrity: Termination (zero tolerance)

**Additional Consequences**:
- Breach notification to affected patients
- Potential criminal charges (if identity theft, etc.)
- Civil liability
- Reporting to professional licensing board (if applicable)

---

### 8.2 Password/Credential Sharing

**Violation**: Sharing login credentials with another person

**Sanction**: 
- First offense, brief sharing: Final written warning
- Sharing for extended period: Termination
- Sharing that led to breach: Termination + liability

**Rationale**: Zero tolerance - undermines all access controls

---

### 8.3 Failed Security Training

**Violation**: Not completing required HIPAA training within 30 days

**Progressive Sanctions**:
- 30 days: Reminder notification
- 45 days: Written warning + training requirement
- 60 days: Access suspended until training complete
- 90 days: Termination

**Note**: Access may be granted provisionally while training scheduled, but must complete within 30 days

---

### 8.4 Phishing/Social Engineering Victim

**Situation**: Employee fell for phishing, compromised credentials

**Sanction**:
- First time, reported immediately: Retraining only (no discipline)
- Failed to report: Written warning
- Repeated failures: Escalating discipline

**Rationale**: Encourage reporting, but require better vigilance

---

### 8.5 Lost/Stolen Device

**Violation**: Lost device with ePHI, not reported within required time

**Determination Factors**:
- Device encrypted? (reduces risk)
- Reported when? (<1 hour = good, >24 hours = bad)
- How lost? (stolen vs. left at restaurant)
- ePHI sensitivity on device

**Typical Sanctions**:
- Encrypted device, reported <1 hour: Retraining only
- Unencrypted device or late reporting: Written warning
- Gross negligence: Termination
- Plus: Cost of breach notification (if required)

---

### 8.6 Disabling Security Controls

**Violation**: Attempting to disable or bypass security features

**Examples**:
- Disabling antivirus
- Trying to bypass MFA
- Attempting to delete audit logs
- Modifying security configurations

**Sanction**: Immediate termination (zero tolerance)

**Rationale**: Intentional undermining of security is never acceptable

---

### 8.7 Failure to Report Security Incident

**Violation**: Knew about security incident but didn't report

**Determination Factors**:
- Severity of unreported incident
- Delay in reporting
- Reason for not reporting

**Typical Sanctions**:
- Minor incident, short delay: Written warning
- Significant incident: Suspension or termination
- Attempted cover-up: Immediate termination

**Rationale**: Duty to report is critical for breach response

---

## 9. APPEAL PROCESS

### 9.1 Right to Appeal

**Employees May Appeal**:
- Any sanction Level 2 or higher
- Termination decisions
- Suspension decisions

**Cannot Appeal**:
- Verbal warnings
- Mandatory training requirements

---

### 9.2 Appeal Procedure

**Step 1: Submit Appeal**
- **Deadline**: Within 10 business days of sanction notice
- **Format**: Written appeal letter
- **Submitted To**: [Appeal review committee or executive]
- **Content**: 
  - Description of violation
  - Why sanction is unjust or excessive
  - Mitigating circumstances
  - Supporting evidence

**Step 2: Appeal Review**
- **Reviewers**: Executive not involved in original decision
- **Timeline**: Decision within 15 business days
- **Process**: 
  - Review original investigation
  - Review employee's appeal
  - May interview involved parties
  - May request additional investigation

**Step 3: Appeal Decision**
- [ ] Uphold original sanction
- [ ] Modify sanction (reduce or increase)
- [ ] Overturn sanction

**Decision is Final**: No further appeals

---

### 9.3 Pending Appeal

**During Appeal**:
- Sanction is stayed (not applied) EXCEPT:
  - Access restrictions (if security risk)
  - Termination (employee on administrative leave)
- Employee continues work (if not terminated)
- Investigation continues if needed

## 11. MITIGATING AND AGGRAVATING FACTORS

### 11.1 Mitigating Factors (May Reduce Sanction)

**Consider**:
- Self-reported violation
- First offense
- Excellent employment record
- Took immediate corrective action
- Cooperated fully with investigation
- No actual harm resulted
- Genuine mistake/confusion
- Inadequate training contributed

**Effect**: May reduce sanction by one level

---

### 11.2 Aggravating Factors (May Increase Sanction)

**Consider**:
- Repeated violations
- Attempted concealment
- Refused to cooperate
- Actual harm to patients
- Malicious intent
- Financial gain motive
- Violation of trust
- Leadership position (held to higher standard)

**Effect**: May increase sanction by one or more levels

---

## 12. ZERO TOLERANCE VIOLATIONS

### Immediate Termination (No Progressive Discipline)

**These violations result in immediate termination**:

1. **Intentional Unauthorized ePHI Access**
   - Accessing celebrity/VIP patient records
   - Snooping on friend/family/ex-spouse records
   - Accessing records out of curiosity (not job-related)
   - Accessing records for blackmail, stalking, etc.

2. **ePHI Disclosure**
   - Sharing ePHI with unauthorized persons
   - Selling patient data
   - Posting ePHI on social media
   - Disclosing to media or competitors

3. **System Tampering**
   - Deleting or modifying audit logs
   - Disabling security controls
   - Creating backdoor access
   - Installing malware or unauthorized software

4. **Identity Theft or Fraud**
   - Using patient information for personal gain
   - Creating fake accounts with patient data
   - Financial fraud using ePHI

5. **Retaliation**
   - Retaliating against employee who reported violation
   - Intimidating witnesses
   - Obstructing investigation

**Additional Consequences**:
- Law enforcement notification
- Professional licensing board notification
- Civil lawsuit (company may seek damages)
- Criminal prosecution (government may prosecute)

## 13. NON-PUNITIVE RESPONSES

### 13.1 When Discipline is Not Appropriate

**Good Faith Reporting**:
- Employee reports their own mistake immediately
- No pattern of violations
- Genuine accident or confusion
- No actual harm resulted

**Response**: 
- Thank employee for reporting
- Retrain on correct procedure
- No formal discipline
- Documented for trend analysis only

**Rationale**: Encourage reporting, safety culture

---

### 13.2 System/Process Issues

**When Violation Due to**:
- Unclear procedures
- System malfunction
- Inadequate training
- Conflicting guidance
- Impossible compliance

**Response**:
- Fix the system/process
- Update procedures
- Improve training
- No employee sanction
- Documented for improvement

**Rationale**: Don't punish employees for organizational failures

---

## 14. SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES

### 14.1 Emergency Access

**Situation**: Employee violated normal access procedures during genuine emergency

**Evaluation**:
- Was it a real emergency?
- Was the access necessary for patient care?
- Were proper procedures followed after emergency?
- Was incident reported properly?

**Typical Response**:
- If genuine emergency and properly reported: No sanction
- If emergency but not reported: Training only
- If fake emergency: Normal discipline process

---

### 14.2 Mandated Reporter Situations

**Situation**: Employee believes they must report patient information (abuse, danger, etc.)

**Evaluation**:
- Was employee a mandated reporter?
- Was the disclosure required by law?
- Were proper procedures followed?
- Was Security Officer or Legal consulted first?

**Typical Response**:
- If legally required: No sanction
- If uncertain but acted in good faith: Education only
- If improper disclosure: Depends on circumstances

**Note**: When in doubt, consult Legal or Security Officer BEFORE disclosure

## 17. REMEDIATION AND RETRAINING

### 17.1 Mandatory Retraining

**When Required**:
- After any violation
- Before return from suspension
- As condition of probation
- Before access restored

**Content**:
- Review of violated policy
- Why policy exists
- Proper procedures
- Case studies of violations
- Consequences of future violations

**Completion**:
- Must pass quiz (90%+ score)
- Sign acknowledgment
- Meet with Security Officer
- Documented in training records

---

### 17.2 Probation Requirements

**During Probation Period**:
- Enhanced monitoring of account activity
- Weekly check-in with manager
- Monthly review with Security Officer
- All actions logged with high-risk flag
- Any violation during probation = termination

**Successful Completion**:
- No further violations during probation
- All required training completed
- Demonstrated understanding
- Probation lifted
- Regular monitoring resumed

**Documentation**: Probation completion certificate

## 19. RETALIATION PROHIBITION

### 19.1 Protected Activities

**Employees May Not Be Sanctioned For**:
- Reporting security incidents (good faith)
- Reporting policy violations
- Reporting illegal activity
- Cooperating with investigations
- Filing HIPAA complaints with HHS
- Testifying in HIPAA proceedings

---

### 19.2 Retaliation is Sanctionable

**Retaliation Includes**:
- Terminating employee who reported violation
- Demoting employee who cooperated with investigation
- Creating hostile work environment
- Negative performance review based on reporting

**Sanction for Retaliation**:
- Manager/supervisor who retaliates: Termination
- Company stance: Zero tolerance

**Protection**:
- Employees may report retaliation to HR or HHS
- Separate investigation conducted
- Strong protections in place
